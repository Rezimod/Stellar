# Stellar — Pitch Video Script (3 minutes)

## (0:00–0:20) — The Buyer
[Screen: Astroman storefront in Tbilisi → cut to a freshly unboxed 8-inch Dobsonian on a dining table]

"Every week, somebody walks out of Astroman in Tbilisi with their first telescope. They get home, set it up, and hit the same wall: it's 9pm, the scope is pointed at the ceiling, and they have no idea what to look at tonight."

## (0:20–0:50) — Tonight's Sky
[Screen: open Stellar → /sky → planet positions, 7-day forecast, dark window highlighted]

"Stellar is the companion app for that buyer. Open it, and tonight's sky is right there — for your exact coordinates. Saturn is up at 22°. The dark window opens at 21:47. Cloud cover stays under 20% until 1am. ASTRA, our AI astronomer, suggests pointing the new scope at the Cassini division."

## (0:50–1:20) — Photograph It, Verify It
[Screen: user steps outside → phone camera → capture → "Analyzing sky + photo…" → verification badge → "Sealing your discovery…" → cNFT preview]

"They go outside. Take the shot. Stellar verifies it: Claude Vision confirms it's actually Saturn, the EXIF GPS matches, and a deterministic hash binds the observation to the exact sky condition at that moment. Then it gets sealed on Solana as a compressed NFT — five-thousandths of a cent per mint. The proof they observed is permanent and theirs."

## (1:20–1:50) — Earn, Redeem
[Screen: success screen with Stars awarded → wallet balance → marketplace → Barlow lens with discount → cart]

"They earn Stars, an SPL token, deposited automatically to a wallet they never had to set up. Stars are spendable at Astroman on a real Barlow lens, real eyepieces, real moon lamps. The closed loop: they observed, the app verified, they got rewarded, they spent it on more astronomy. Real product, real economy."

## (1:50–2:10) — ASTRA AI
[Screen: /chat → "what can I see tonight?" → ASTRA streams a response with live planet positions and Bortle estimate]

"ASTRA isn't a chatbot reading from a script. It calls live astronomy APIs — actual planet altitudes, actual cloud cover for the user's coordinates, actual Bortle estimates. It tells you what you can see, not what some article says you might."

## (2:10–2:30) — Every Phone Is a Sensor
[Screen: /network → live map dotted with observer nodes pulsing in different cities]

"Stellar is a DePIN. Every smartphone running it is a sky sensor — GPS, timestamp, photo, weather hash. Each verified observation is a cNFT on Solana, building a public, immutable record of what was visible from where. 60,000 Astroman followers in Georgia today. Same protocol scales globally."

## (2:30–2:50) — Distribution
[Screen: Rezi at Astroman → physical inventory shelves → social posts in Georgian → schools photo]

"This isn't a hackathon project hoping a community appears. Astroman has a physical store in Tbilisi, ~$150K in telescope inventory, and a 60,000-person audience already. Every customer is a Stellar user. Every Stellar observer is a future Astroman customer. International expansion is one regional dealer per region — Celestron for the Americas, Levenhuk for Europe."

## (2:50–3:00) — Close
[Screen: Stellar logo + URL]

"Stellar. Astronomy, on chain. Live on Solana mainnet."
